Narrative theory

Every story has
an emotional shape.

Based on Kurt Vonnegut's research, BookScope classifies every book into one of six fundamental emotional trajectories — automatically.

Rags to Riches rise
Steady rise from hardship to triumph. Oliver Twist, Jane Eyre, The Alchemist.
Riches to Rags fall
Unrelenting descent. Requiem for a Dream, Of Mice and Men.
Man in a Hole fall-rise
Trouble found, then escaped. The Hobbit, The Martian, Room.
Icarus rise-fall
Heights of ambition, then catastrophe. Crime & Punishment, Macbeth.
Cinderella complex
Rise, setback, then triumphant finale. The archetypal happy ending.
Oedipus complex
False hope, partial recovery, final ruin. Hamlet, King Lear.
