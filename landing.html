Narrative theory

Every story has
an emotional shape.

Based on Kurt Vonnegut's narrative arc research, BookScope classifies every book into one of six fundamental emotional trajectories.

Rags to Riches rise
Steady rise from hardship to triumph. Found in Oliver Twist, Jane Eyre, The Alchemist.
Riches to Rags fall
Unrelenting descent. Found in Requiem for a Dream, Of Mice and Men.
Man in a Hole fall-rise
Trouble found, then escaped. The most universal story shape.
Icarus rise-fall
Heights of ambition, then catastrophe. Crime & Punishment, Breaking Bad.
Cinderella complex
Rise, setback, then triumphant finale. The archetypal happy ending.
Oedipus complex
False hope, partial recovery, final ruin. Hamlet, King Lear, Macbeth.
Process

From raw text
to emotional map.

01
Upload or link
Drop a .txt or .epub file, paste a URL, or try the built-in demo. BookScope handles encoding detection automatically.
02
Chunk & analyze
The text is split into chunks. Each chunk is scored across 8 NRC emotions — anger, anticipation, disgust, fear, joy, sadness, surprise, trust.
03
Classify arc
A valence series is derived from emotional scores and compared to 6 canonical arc templates using mean absolute error matching.
04
Render insight
Results appear as Quick Insight cards or an 8-tab full analysis. Save to library, compare with other books, or share a permanent link.
